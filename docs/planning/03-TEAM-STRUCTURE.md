## Core SCRUM Team

### Product Owner

**Overview**: Owns product vision, manages backlog, maximizes business value

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Define and communicate product vision and strategy
- Manage and prioritize product backlog
- Write and refine user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Make decisions on features, scope, and release planning
- Represent stakeholder interests (customers, business, compliance)
- Accept or reject work results
- Maximize ROI and ensure market fit
- Conduct user research and gather feedback
- Monitor product metrics and KPIs

**Required Qualifications**:
- **Education**: Bachelor's in Business, Accounting, or related field; MBA preferred
- **Experience**: 5+ years in accounting/finance industry
- **Skills**:
  - Deep understanding of accounting processes and pain points
  - Proven track record in product management
  - Strong business acumen and analytical skills
  - Excellent communication and stakeholder management
  - SCRUM Product Owner certification (CSPO or equivalent)
  - Familiarity with Romanian and EU accounting regulations

**Domain Knowledge**:
- Financial accounting, bookkeeping, tax compliance
- Romanian fiscal regulations
- EU accounting standards (IFRS)
- GDPR and data privacy
- SaaS business models

**Tools**: JIRA, Confluence, Aha!, ProductBoard, Google Analytics, Mixpanel

**Reporting**: CEO
**Location**: Bucharest (hybrid)
**Salary Range**: €60,000 - €80,000/year

## Team Culture & Values

### Core Values

1. **Customer First**: Everything we build must create value for our customers
2. **Quality Over Speed**: Ship when it's right, not when it's rushed
3. **Transparency**: Open communication, no silos, share knowledge
4. **Continuous Learning**: Invest in growth, embrace feedback
5. **Ownership**: Take responsibility, see tasks through to completion
6. **Collaboration**: No rockstars, winning together as a team
7. **Innovation**: Challenge assumptions, experiment, embrace failure as learning

### SCRUM Culture

- **Psychological Safety**: Safe to ask questions, admit mistakes, propose ideas
- **Sustainable Pace**: No regular overtime, maintain work-life balance
- **Inspect and Adapt**: Regular retrospectives, act on improvements
- **Definition of Done**: Shared understanding of quality standards
- **Cross-Functional**: Team has all skills needed, minimize handoffs
- **Empowerment**: Team self-organizes, makes technical decisions

### Engineering Practices

- **Code Reviews**: All code reviewed by at least 2 peers
- **Test-Driven Development**: Write tests first when appropriate
- **Pair Programming**: For complex features, knowledge sharing
- **Documentation**: Code should be self-documenting, complex logic explained
- **Refactoring**: Allocate 20% sprint capacity to technical debt
- **Continuous Integration**: Automated tests on every commit
- **Blameless Postmortems**: Learn from failures without punishment

### Communication

- **Daily Standup**: 15 minutes, 9:00 AM EET (async on Slack for remote)
- **Slack Guidelines**:
  - Public channels by default
  - Respect offline time (no expectation to respond outside working hours)
  - Use threads, emojis for quick reactions
- **Documentation**: Confluence for all project documentation
- **Video Calls**: Zoom for meetings, cameras on encouraged
- **Async First**: Document decisions, don't rely solely on meetings

### Work Environment

- **Flexible Hours**: Core hours 10 AM - 4 PM, flexible start/end
- **Remote-Friendly**: Remote work available, office in Bucharest
- **Equipment**: Macbook/ThinkPad, external monitor, choice of tools
- **Learning Budget**: €1,000/year per person for courses, conferences
- **Team Events**: Monthly team lunch, quarterly offsite
- **Health & Wellness**: Health insurance, gym membership

### Career Development

- **Growth Plans**: Individual development plans reviewed quarterly
- **Mentorship**: Junior/mid-level paired with senior mentors
- **Internal Mobility**: Opportunities to move between roles/squads
- **Conference Attendance**: Support speaking and attending conferences
- **Certifications**: Company pays for relevant certifications

## Organizational Policies

### Onboarding

**Week 1**:
- IT setup (laptop, accounts, access)
- SCRUM training and team introduction
- Product overview and demo
- Review documentation and codebase
- Shadow team members

**Week 2-4**:
- Start with small, well-defined tasks
- Pair programming with team members
- Attend all SCRUM ceremonies
- Complete compliance training (GDPR, security)
- First code contribution

**Month 2-3**:
- Take on larger features
- Present in sprint review
- Participate in architecture discussions

### Performance Reviews

- **Frequency**: Quarterly check-ins, annual formal review
- **360 Feedback**: Input from peers, SCRUM Master, Product Owner
- **Growth Plans**: Career development goals set quarterly
- **Compensation Review**: Annual salary review based on performance and market

### Conflict Resolution

1. **Team Level**: Discuss in retrospective or 1-on-1
2. **SCRUM Master**: Mediate if team cannot resolve
3. **Management**: Escalate to CTO/CEO if needed
4. **HR Partner**: Involve external HR consultant for serious issues
